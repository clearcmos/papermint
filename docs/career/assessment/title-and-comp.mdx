## Current Title

> Senior IT Specialist

## Why It No Longer Fits

"Specialist" implies deep expertise in a narrow area. You operate across 25+ systems. The title caps your compensation at IT support bands and misrepresents your actual contribution.

## The Quebec "Engineer" Constraint

In Quebec, the title "engineer" is legally protected in **both English and French** under the Engineers Act (Sections 22 and 26). The protected words include "ingénieur", "engineer", "génie", "ingénierie", and "engineering." The OIQ actively enforces this — they successfully sued Microsoft in 2005 over "Microsoft Certified Systems Engineer," and they contacted your employer directly about title usage. There is no software/IT industry exemption, and unlike Alberta (which carved out a software engineer exception in 2023), Quebec has not created one. This is not a theoretical risk — it's an active enforcement reality.

This means any title containing "engineer" is off the table in Quebec unless you hold an OIQ permit. However, "architect" is not a protected term — it's legally safe and carries higher market value.

## Recommended Titles (Ranked)

**1. Automation & Identity Architect** — The most accurate positioning. Reflects your core professional work: designing multi-system automated workflows across identity, cloud, and security platforms (Okta, GCP, Google Workspace, Kandji, SentinelOne). IAM Architect roles pay $95-200k USD. Okta is your strongest recruiter keyword — Okta + Terraform is a genuinely hard-to-find combination. Quebec-safe.

**2. Infrastructure Architect** — Broader search magnet with the highest general volume. Montreal range: $103K-$177K CAD. US range: $141K-$221K USD. Defensible given your NixOS fleet, Terraform IaC, and cloud functions, but less precise than Identity & Automation Architect. Quebec-safe.

**3. Senior Infrastructure Architect** — Same as above with seniority qualifier. "Senior" reflects 15 years of experience. Quebec-safe.

**4. Platform Architect** — Emphasizes the internal platform work (iss-utils as a 400+ tool platform, claude-plugins as a distribution system). Good for companies building internal developer platforms. Quebec-safe.

**5. IT Automation Architect** — Conservative but accurate. Keeps the IT framing your company may be comfortable with while signaling architecture-level work. Easiest internal sell. Quebec-safe.

**Why not AIOps?** AIOps in the job market means ML-driven observability and monitoring (Prometheus, Grafana, Splunk, anomaly detection, Kubernetes). That's not your skill set. Your AI usage is as a development methodology, not an operations monitoring capability. Positioning as AIOps would put you in interviews asking about observability stacks and ML frameworks you don't have.

## Titles to Avoid

- **Any title with "Engineer"** — legally protected in Quebec; the OIQ has already contacted your employer
- **"Specialist"** without qualifier — you've outgrown generic "IT Specialist"
- **"Administrator"** — undersells the automation and platform work
- **"AI Engineer"** — you're not building ML models; you're using AI to build IT infrastructure. Also triggers Quebec engineer title protection.
- **"AIOps Specialist/Engineer"** — AIOps in the market means ML-driven monitoring and observability (Kubernetes, Prometheus, Splunk). Your AI usage is a development methodology, not an operations monitoring capability. Would set wrong expectations in interviews.
- **"Prompt Engineer"** — too narrow, undersells your infrastructure work
- **"MLOps Engineer"** — requires ML pipeline expertise that isn't your focus
- **"Solutions Architect"** — too vague, sounds like consulting

### Why the Montreal Market Caps You

- Montreal pays 15-25% less than Toronto for equivalent roles, and 40-50% less than US market
- "IT" titles in Quebec carry lower comp bands than "Software" or "Platform" titles — even when the work is equivalent
- Most HR departments benchmark against title, not output. If the title says "IT," the comp bands are IT comp bands.
- The AI-augmented productivity angle is a real differentiator, but almost no company has a compensation framework for it yet

### Leverage Plays

**1. Infrastructure Architect positioning (biggest framing shift).** "Infrastructure Architect" is a well-established title with the highest salary ceiling in Montreal ($103K-$177K CAD). It accurately describes your work — Terraform IaC, identity infrastructure, cloud functions, multi-system automation — and moves you out of IT support comp bands into architecture comp bands. Quebec-safe (not OIQ-protected).

**2. Remote US companies (biggest multiplier).** Your skill set — Okta + Terraform + Google Workspace + MDM + Python + AI-augmented automation at scale — is in high demand for US IT/security teams. A US remote Infrastructure Architect role at $141-221k USD translates to 190-300k+ CAD.

**3. The iss-utils repo as portfolio.** 400+ production scripts, cloud functions in production, data migrations that saved projects. This is more concrete output than most candidates show in interviews. Combined with the claude-plugins AI platform architecture, this portfolio demonstrates both traditional IT depth and AI-forward capability.

### Bottom Line

The internal path delivered a 2.5% raise — well below the gap. Externally in Montreal, Infrastructure Architect roles range $103-177k. Remote US is $141-221k USD. The gap between 105k and 177k+ isn't about skill — it's about positioning and market access. The Infrastructure Architect framing is the key that unlocks the higher comp bands.
